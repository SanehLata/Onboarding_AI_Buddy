# 30-60-90 Day Plan for New Engineers

**Last Updated:** January 2025
**Owner:** Engineering People Team
**Tags:** onboarding, 30-day-plan, goals, ramp-up, new-joiner

---

## Overview

This plan gives you a clear roadmap for your first 90 days at TechCorp Engineering. It is a guide, not a strict rulebook. Discuss adjustments with your manager in your weekly 1:1s.

---

## First 30 Days — Learn & Absorb

Your goal in the first 30 days is to understand the system, not to change it.

### Week 1: Environment & Team
- Complete `day1_checklist.md`
- Set up your full development environment — see `tools_setup.md`
- Read the team's Confluence space, focusing on architecture and runbooks
- Attend all team ceremonies — standup, sprint planning, retrospective
- Shadow your buddy on their daily work — ask questions, take notes

### Week 2: Codebase Exploration
- Clone all team repositories and read the READMEs
- Trace a request end-to-end through your team's primary service
- Read the last 5 architecture decision records (ADRs) in Confluence
- Review the last 3 post-incident reports
- Pick up a `good-first-issue` Jira ticket — something small and contained

### Week 3: First Contribution
- Complete your first pull request — even a small bug fix or documentation update counts
- Get your PR reviewed and merged
- Attend your first on-call shadowing session with a senior engineer
- Read `logging_standards.md` and `deployment_guide.md`

### Week 4: Consolidation
- Complete 30-day review with your manager
- Identify 3 areas you feel confident in and 3 areas you want to develop
- Set your first Jira sprint goal — something achievable in 2 weeks
- Begin preparing for on-call rotation (starts at day 30)

**30-Day Success Markers:**
- [ ] First PR merged
- [ ] Can explain your team's primary service end-to-end
- [ ] All system access provisioned and working
- [ ] On-call rotation joined

---

## Days 31–60 — Contribute & Build

Your goal is to deliver independently on well-scoped tasks and build confidence in the codebase.

### Focus Areas
- Pick up medium-complexity Jira tickets independently
- Write unit tests for any new code you produce
- Begin participating actively in code reviews — both giving and receiving feedback
- Contribute to at least one Confluence documentation update
- Attend your first on-call rotation with support from your buddy

### Goals
- Deliver at least one complete feature or bug fix from Jira to production
- Run your first production deployment — shadowed by a senior engineer
- Present a brief tech share (15 minutes) to the team on something you learned
- Identify and propose one improvement to the team's processes or codebase

**60-Day Success Markers:**
- [ ] Delivered a feature end to end
- [ ] Completed first on-call rotation
- [ ] Run a production deployment (shadowed)
- [ ] Presented a tech share

---

## Days 61–90 — Own & Lead

Your goal is to operate with increasing independence and take ownership of a defined area.

### Focus Areas
- Take ownership of a specific service, feature area, or Jira epic
- Lead a sprint planning session or backlog refinement
- Run a production deployment independently
- Mentor the next new joiner if one joins during this period
- Begin contributing to architectural discussions — your fresh perspective is valuable

### Goals
- Define and execute a 2-week sprint goal independently
- Propose and document one ADR (Architecture Decision Record) for a change you want to make
- Achieve sign-off for production SSH access
- Complete your 90-day review with your manager and set Q1 OKRs

**90-Day Success Markers:**
- [ ] Operating independently on the team
- [ ] Owns a defined area of the codebase or system
- [ ] Production SSH access approved
- [ ] 90-day review completed and OKRs set
